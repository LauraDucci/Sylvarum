Enhancing vegetable growth via electromagnetic stimuli, to accelerate a transition to a more sustainable and resilient way of producing food.

WE HAVE A PROBLEM

By 2050, the world population will reach 9.5 billion people and food production will need to increase by 70% to ensure its survival. Not only are we not prepared to face such a scenario, but we have as our starting point an already obsolete food production system.

In terms of vegetable cultivation, thousands of kilometers separate producers from costumers, which forces the use of long-distance means of transport, making the product more expensive and generating emissions. This, added to the intensive use of pesticides, has a negative impact on the environment. About half of the total production is lost during this process, due to external factors such as frosts or droughts, also in harvest, transport and even at the store shelf waiting to be bought.

The limitations imposed by the COVID-19 pandemic have aggravated those weaknesses dramatically. Incalculable tons were lost due to the inability of harvesters to travel, supply chains were cut off, and many countries lost the access to import or export their products.

If we add global warming and exponential population growth to all this, the situation becomes similar to having a time bomb in our hands. Sustainable and resilient plant production systems need to be developed.

WHAT'S HAPPENING SO FAR

With all this into account, hydroponic farming is a very attractive alternative to standard methods. It is used to grow plants using mineral solutions instead of agricultural soil. This industry has multiple advantages:

  • It is a system that can be installed in cities, avoiding the long-distance transport of fresh products which provides predictability.
  • It maximizes the efficiency of resources, such as water and space.
  • The quality of its products is superior compared to soil crops, from a nutritional point of view, appearance, cleanliness and, above all, duration.
  • The use of greenhouses and shades allows to produce in all types of landscape, climates and times of the year, being able to federalize the production of this type of food.
  • It is a highly environmentally friendly, it does not use herbicides or pesticides, reducing environmental pollution.

These factors make hydroponics an attractive, functional and sustainable option but, being a developing market, it is not fully exploited technologically.

There are important improvement opportunities that need to become true in order to stablish hydroponic farming at the industrial field. It is exactly there where Sylvarum introduces itself as a technology whose action will change the game rules on big scales productions.

FASTER, BETTER, FASTER, STRONGER

With the fusion of hydroponics and electroculture, Sylvarum proposes to use biophysical stimuli to hack into the crops metabolic system and thus increase their yield. By exposing the plant to certain electric and magnetic fields, we can control the exchange of ions with the aquatic growth medium, stimulating its nutritional capacity and accelerating its growth.

In addition, this technology can be complemented with the three most developed branches of industrial improvements: energy optimization by the light source, artificial intelligence applied to the greenhouse conditions, and robotic automation.

This advance is expected to considerably reduce time until harvest, increasing the number of growing cycles and reducing costs for producers, especially in energy and equipment depreciation, meaning a significant improvement in the profitability of this agricultural system. Following the rise of this market, Sylvarum seeks to add a new concept to production, through the implementation of cutting-edge technology.
